Cristian Apolinar

Doctoral Candidate | Criminology, Law & Society | University of California, Irvine

Situated at the intersection of criminology and urban studies, my research investigates the community-level consequences of proactive policing strategies, focusing on how place-based and intensive enforcement shape neighborhood conditions and equity over time. By employing a combination of longitudinal and spatial methodologies, I aggregate incident- and individual-level data to census-defined geographic units to evaluate policy effects and monitor changes in neighborhood characteristics over time, including poverty rates, educational attainment, and racial and ethnic composition.
